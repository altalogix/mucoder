The chord fitting algorithm

 

In case you wonder how it’s done, here is a short summary of the geometrical chord fitting algorithm:

 

1.First it selects all candidate chords that match your chord filter (dyad, triad, seventh) and that would fit the space at the root note you are pointing at. A fit means that all notes of the chord fit onto colored cells, therefore are part of the scale/key. These candidate chords are taken from the list of chords present in the chord combobox after applying the chord filter.
 

2.Then it ranks those candidate chords a) by number of notes and then b) by the average city-block distance that their constituent cells have between eachother in the space. This is the average of the pythagorean distances between their cell centers. Chords with a greater number of notes rank higher than chord with a lower number of notes. If two chords have the same number of notes, the one with the smallest city-block distance between its cells wins.
 

3.If there is only one top ranking chord, this will be the one that is selected.
 

4.If there are two or more candidates that rank equally at the top, then it will alternate between these (each alternation requires a new mouse click or a new midi note-on event).
